Venture Ideation & Entrepreneurship

Strategic Documentation & Business Insights

Unit 1

Q1. Technology Push vs Market Pull

Answer: Technology push happens when a company first creates a new technology and later looks for customers who may need it. The innovation is mainly driven by research and development. For example, Apple introduced Face ID technology in smartphones before many users actually demanded it. Later, people started liking the convenience and security, making it popular.

Market pull happens when companies observe customer problems and then develop products to solve them. For example, Uber was created because people wanted faster and more convenient transportation services. The demand already existed in the market.

The difference: Technology push = "Technology first", whereas Market pull = "Customer need first".

Q2. Business Model Canvas (BMC)

Answer: A Business Model Canvas is a visual framework used to understand how a business works. It explains how a company creates value, delivers it to customers, and earns profit. It contains 9 parts such as:

  • Customer segments
  • Value proposition
  • Revenue streams
  • Key resources

For example, Airbnb uses a simple business model: Customers → Travelers, Value → Affordable accommodation, and Revenue → Commission from bookings. Instead of owning hotels, Airbnb connects hosts and travelers through its platform.

Q3. Significance of Innovation in Entrepreneurship

Answer: Innovation means introducing new ideas, products, or methods to improve business. Innovation is important because it helps businesses survive competition and attract customers. For example, Apple changed the mobile industry with the iPhone by combining internet, music, and touch technology into one device. This innovation made smartphones more useful and popular.

Its primary importance includes:

  • Creates competitive advantage
  • Improves customer experience
  • Increases growth opportunities

Q4. Factors Influencing Innovation

Answer: Innovation depends on many internal and external factors such as:

  • Technology
  • Market demand
  • Government support
  • Competition
  • Financial resources

For example, Tesla grew rapidly because people wanted eco-friendly vehicles, battery technology improved, and governments promoted electric vehicles. These factors encouraged innovation in electric cars.

Q5. Product-Market Fit

Answer: Product-market fit occurs when a product perfectly satisfies customer needs and gains strong demand. For example, WhatsApp became successful because people needed a simple, fast, and low-cost messaging platform. It solved communication problems better than traditional SMS.

Signs of Product-Market Fit:

  • Customers regularly use the product
  • Demand increases naturally
  • Positive feedback and growth occur

Unit 2

Q6. Customer Archetypes & Segmentation

Answer: Customer segmentation means dividing customers into groups based on common characteristics like age, income, interests, or behavior. A customer archetype is an imaginary profile representing the ideal customer.

For example, Nike targets different customer groups such as Athletes, Gym users, and Casual fashion users. Nike designs different products and advertisements for each group, which results in better marketing, deeper understanding of customers, and increased sales.

Q7. Business Models

Answer: A business model explains how a company earns money and delivers value. Notable examples include:

  • Subscription: Netflix uses a subscription-based model. Customers pay monthly fees to access unlimited entertainment content.
  • Freemium: Spotify
  • Marketplace: Amazon
  • Advertisement: YouTube

Q8. Value Proposition

Answer: A value proposition explains why customers should choose a product over competitors. It focuses strictly on Benefits, Problem-solving, and Uniqueness.

For example, Amazon attracts customers through fast delivery, large product variety, and convenient shopping. These features form Amazon's core value proposition.

Q9. Customer Interview & Insights

Answer: Customer interviews help businesses understand customer problems and expectations through direct interaction. For example, Zomato collects ratings and reviews from users to improve restaurant recommendations and delivery services.

Benefits include understanding customer pain points, improving products/services, and reducing overall business risk.

Unit 3

Q10. Customer Value & Customer Centricity

Answer: Customer value means the benefit customers receive from a product. Customer centricity means focusing business decisions around customer satisfaction.

For example, Amazon is customer-centric because it provides easy returns, fast delivery, and personalized recommendations. This directly improves customer satisfaction and loyalty.

Q11. Crossing the Chasm

Answer: Crossing the chasm refers to moving from early users of a product to the mainstream market. Many startups fail at this stage because mainstream customers require trust and proven performance.

For example, Tesla first attracted technology enthusiasts and wealthy buyers. Later, it introduced affordable models to reach normal customers and enter the mass market.

Q12. Market Segmentation

Answer: Market segmentation divides the market into smaller groups with similar needs.

For example, Coca-Cola offers Diet Coke for health-conscious users, Coke Zero for sugar-free preference, and Regular Coke for general consumers. Different products satisfy different customer groups effectively.

Q13. E-commerce vs E-business

Answer: E-commerce involves simply buying and selling products online. For example, Flipkart allows users to purchase products online.

E-business includes all business activities conducted using the internet such as inventory management, marketing, and customer support. For example, Amazon not only sells products online but also manages logistics, cloud services, and digital payments.

Unit 4

Q14. Rapid Prototyping

Answer: Rapid prototyping means quickly creating a sample or model of a product before final production.

For example, Dyson created thousands of vacuum cleaner prototypes before launching the final product. Benefits include saving time, identifying errors early, and improving design quality.

Q15. MVP (Minimum Viable Product)

Answer: An MVP is the simplest version of a product with only essential features.

For example, Facebook initially launched only for Harvard students with limited features. After positive feedback, more features were added. Its importance lies in testing ideas cheaply, collecting user feedback, and reducing business risk.

Q16. Lean Startup

Answer: Lean startup is a method where businesses quickly build products, test them, and improve them continuously. It strictly follows the Build → Measure → Learn cycle.

For example, Dropbox first released a simple demo video to test customer interest before building the complete software infrastructure.

Q17. Mockups and Wireframing

Answer: Wireframes show the structure of a product, while mockups show the final visual design.

For example, Instagram designers first created wireframes of app screens before developing the final interface. This helps visualize ideas, improves planning, and saves development cost.

Q18. Anatomy of an Experience Map

Answer: An experience map visually represents the customer journey with a product or service. It includes customer actions, emotions, problems, and interactions.

For example, Uber maps the complete customer journey: Booking → Driver tracking → Ride → Payment → Rating. This systematic breakdown helps improve user experience at every single stage.
